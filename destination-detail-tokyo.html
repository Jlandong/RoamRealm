Tokyo, Japan

Overview

The Tokyo Imperial Palace is the residence of Japan's Emperor, located in central Tokyo on the former site of Edo Castle. Surrounded by moats and gardens, most of the palace is closed to the public, except on special occasions like New Year and the Emperor’s Birthday. Nearby attractions include the Nijubashi Bridge and the East Gardens, which are open to visitors.

Highlights

  • Nijubashi Bridge – A picturesque double-arched stone bridge leading to the palace grounds.
  • Fushimi-yagura – A surviving guard tower from the Edo period.
  • Main Palace Buildings (Kyūden) – Official residence and administrative buildings (viewable from afar).
  • Enjoy Tokyo’s world-class sushi and ramen

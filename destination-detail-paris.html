The Louvre

Overview

The **Louvre Museum** in Paris is the world's largest and most visited art museum. Originally a royal palace, it became a public museum in 1793. It's famous for the *Mona Lisa* and features a modern glass pyramid entrance. The museum holds over 480,000 works of art from around the world.

Highlights

  • Mona Lisa – Leonardo da Vinci’s iconic portrait with the mysterious smile
  • Venus de Milo – An ancient Greek statue of the goddess Aphrodite, known for its beauty and missing arms.
  • Winged Victory of Samothrace – A dramatic Hellenistic sculpture of Nike, the goddess of victory.
